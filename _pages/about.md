I am a fourth-year Ph.D. student in Computer Science at [Washington University in St. Louis](https://washu.edu/), advised by Prof. [Chenyang Lu](https://www.cse.wustl.edu/~lu/) in the [AI for Health Institute](https://aihealth.wustl.edu/). My research lies at the intersection of machine learning for healthcare, multimodal AI, and clinical decision support, with a focus on developing trustworthy and uncertainty-aware models for real-world clinical applications.

Prior to my Ph.D., I earned my Bachelor’s degree with a double major in Computer Science and Mathematics from Washington University in St. Louis, graduating summa cum laude. During my undergraduate studies, I conducted research under the guidance of Prof. Lu on machine learning for healthcare.



### Multimodal AI for Healthcare

In Multimodal AI for Healthcare, I develop predictive models that integrate wearable sensing data, ecological momentary assessments (EMA), unstructured clinical notes, and structured electronic health records (EHR). My work focuses on improving prediction of surgical outcomes, such as persistent post-surgical pain and recovery trajectories, by leveraging diverse data modalities and modeling their interactions.

### Trustworthy and Uncertainty-Aware Machine Learning

In Trustworthy and Uncertainty-Aware Machine Learning, I design methods to quantify uncertainty in both traditional machine learning models and large language models. My research introduces modality-specific uncertainty estimation and reliability-aware fusion strategies, enabling models to assess confidence, improve robustness, and support safer deployment in clinical decision-making.

### Clinical AI and Real-World Impact

In Clinical AI and Real-World Impact, I collaborate with interdisciplinary teams across anesthesiology, neurosurgery, and public health to develop deployable systems. My work spans applications including treatment effect estimation, disease phenotyping, and global health analytics, with the goal of translating machine learning advances into meaningful healthcare improvements.

Through these efforts, I aim to build reliable, interpretable, and clinically actionable AI systems that can support personalized medicine and improve patient outcomes.
